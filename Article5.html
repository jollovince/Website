Odette Quesada’s Music: The Gift That Keeps on Giving

Published November 22, 2023

MANILA, Philippines — If you think only foreign artists or dreamy K-pop idols can dominate the local concert scene, think again. Odette Quesada’s anniversary concert at the Newport Performing Arts Theater last Sept. 1 was already a sold-out, star-studded affair long before the beloved singer-songwriter even stepped on stage.

So it came as no surprise when organizers from AiP (Anything is Possible) Media Production Inc. announced that tickets for her concert repeat at the Samsung Performing Arts Theater in Circuit Makati were also sold out within days. Due to overwhelming demand, an additional show was added—but even that sold out within the same week of ticket release, two months before the event!

Producer, writer, and talent manager Noel Ferrer confirmed the impressive turnout, sharing with a laugh, “We sold out that first date practically overnight—as in!”

When asked how she feels about the continued love and support from Filipino music fans, Odette expressed pure gratitude and joy. “I’m so thankful that people still sing my songs,” she said. “They’ve become the gifts that keep on giving.”

Indeed, her music has stood the test of time. Songs like To Love Again (Sharon Cuneta), Don’t Know What to Do, Don’t Know What to Say and Give Me a Chance (Ric Segreto), I Need You Back and Farewell (Raymond Lauchengco), Till I Met You (Kuh Ledesma), Growing Up (Gary Valenciano), and her own hits Friend of Mine and I Remember You remain beloved by generations of listeners.
