ABOUT

Odette Quesada is a legendary Filipina singer-songwriter whose music has defined the sound of love and romance in Original Pilipino Music (OPM). From the very beginning of her career in the 1980s, Odette Quesada established herself as a prolific songwriter with an exceptional gift for weaving heartfelt lyrics into timeless melodies.

Odette Quesada first gained national attention when she won second prize in the 1982 Metro Manila Popular Music Festival (Metropop) amateur division for her song “Give Me a Chance,” performed by Ric Segreto. In 1983, Odette followed this with a monumental achievement — she clinched the Grand Prize in the professional category at the 6th Metro Manila Popular Music Festival for “Till I Met You,” sung by Kuh Ledesma.

The 1980s became a golden era for the music of Odette Quesada, as her songs were brought to life by some of the country’s biggest and brightest stars. The late Ric Segreto turned “Don’t Know What to Do, Don’t Know What to Say” into a monster hit that remains a classic today. Teen idol Raymond Lauchengco found back-to-back success with “I Need You Back” and “Farewell,” while the iconic youth film Bagets was immortalized by its theme song “Growing Up,” performed by Gary Valenciano — another unforgettable creation from Odette’s pen.

Her music also left an indelible mark on Philippine cinema. Sharon Cuneta, the country’s “Megastar,” recorded “To Love Again” as the theme for her movie of the same title, further cementing Odette’s role as one of OPM’s most sought-after composers.

Yet Odette’s artistry did not stop at writing for others. It wasn’t long before she stepped into the spotlight as a singer, winning hearts with her own unique voice and intimate style. With hits like “Friend of Mine,” “You’re My Home,” “Dito Lang,” and “Wala Nang Nasa Isip,” she proved that she was not only a brilliant songwriter but also a captivating performer whose interpretations carried the same sincerity and depth as her compositions.

In recognition of her decades of influence and impact, Odette Quesada has been the recipient of several lifetime achievement honors:

  • Odette Quesada was awarded the Parangal Levi Celerio Lifetime Achievement Award at the 15th PMPC Star Awards for Music in January 2024.
  • She was also honored as one of the recipients of the Ryan Cayabyab Awards at the 2024 Pinoy Playlist Music Festival, for her excellence and enduring contribution to Filipino music.

My Favorite Story – A Dedication to Her Audience

For Odette Quesada, every song is more than just music — it is a conversation with those who listen. “My Favorite Story” stands as one of her most personal dedications to her audience, a love letter in song to everyone who has ever cherished a memory worth holding on to.

Dedicated to her listeners, “My Favorite Story” is more than a ballad; it is a gift — an invitation to reflect, to feel, and to treasure the stories that shape who we are. In offering this song, Odette Quesada once again proves that her music is not just heard, but deeply felt, leaving every listener with a memory to call their own.
